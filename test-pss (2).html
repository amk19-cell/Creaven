Perceived Stress Scale

PSS-10 - Cohen, Kamarck and Mermelstein, 1983

The Perceived Stress Scale is the most widely used psychological instrument for measuring the perception of stress. It assesses the degree to which situations in one's life are appraised as stressful - particularly relevant for entertainment industry workers facing unpredictable schedules, high performance demands, and intense interpersonal pressure.
