SWLS — Diener, Emmons, Larsen & Griffin, 1985

The SWLS is the most widely used measure of cognitive life satisfaction in psychology and public health research. It assesses a person's global judgment of their life — not emotions or feelings, but how they evaluate their life as a whole. In the creative industry, life satisfaction is particularly vulnerable to the volatility of creative careers, income instability, and the gap between artistic ambition and reality.
