CBI — Kristensen, Borritz, Villadsen & Christensen, 2005

The CBI is a validated 19-item measure of burnout across three dimensions: Personal Burnout, Work-Related Burnout, and Client-Related Burnout. Unlike generic burnout scales, it separates burnout from its source — a critical distinction in the creative industry where professional, personal, and client-related exhaustion often overlap and mask each other.

What to expect during your consultation: Your psychologist will ask you 19 questions about your experience of fatigue, exhaustion, and emotional depletion. Some questions relate to your general state, some to your work specifically, and some to your relationship with the people you work with or serve. The process takes approximately 15–20 minutes.

Scoring: The CBI uses a 5-point frequency/intensity scale. Scores per subscale range from 0 to 100. A score above 50 is considered clinically significant for that dimension of burnout.
